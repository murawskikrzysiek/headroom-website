Setup & Mic

Do I need a measurement microphone?

#

For meaningful absolute SPL you want a calibrated measurement mic, but Audita works with any CoreAudio input device. A mic-correction profile compensates for the capsule's frequency response so the reading reflects the room, not the mic. Without calibration the figures are relative — still useful for tracking consistency and trends over a session.

Does Audita record my audio?

#

No. Audio from your input device is analysed in real time to derive a level number and is never recorded, saved, or transmitted. Nothing leaves your Mac. Audita requests microphone permission only so macOS will let it read the input level.

Which input should I use?

#

Pick any individual input channel from your interface in Audita's settings — not just the default stereo pair. A dedicated measurement mic on its own input is ideal; a talkback mic also works for relative tracking.

Calibration

How does target-level calibration work?

#

The pink-noise wizard plays a reference signal and walks you through setting your monitor controller to a specific dBA target. Audita records the knob-position note, the target value, and the measured SPL — one calibration per output device. From then on, the Monitoring Discipline tracker knows where "on target" is.

What is Monitoring Discipline?

#

A live measure of how consistently you actually monitor at your calibrated reference. It shows as an on-target % chip in the menu bar with a green / yellow / orange ladder, plus a three-segment under / on / over bar. It's the antidote to the monitor knob quietly drifting over a long day.

Hearing dose

What standard does the hearing dose use?

#

Audita accumulates dose using the NIOSH REL: an 85 dBA reference, a 3 dB exchange rate, and an 80 dBA accumulation threshold — the most protective mainstream occupational standard. Peak monitoring follows EU Directive 2003/10/EC (135 / 137 / 140 dBC action values, 115 dBA instantaneous ceiling).

What's the Pressure Bloom in my menu bar?

#

It's the menu-bar indicator that shows two things at once: live SPL (the arc count) and today's accumulated dose tier (the colour — green / yellow / orange / red). One glance tells you both how loud it is right now and how much of your day's safe exposure you've used.

When should I use Conservative mode?

#

Conservative mode accumulates dose using max(dBA, dBC) rather than dBA alone. Turn it on for bass-heavy work — sub-mixing, kick tracking, live sound — where low-frequency energy contributes more exposure than A-weighting credits.

Lyra pairing

How does "Send to Lyra" work?

#

After a target-level calibration, click "Send to Lyra…" and pick a slot. Audita writes the calibrated dB SPL into one of Lyra's three reference slots via the lyra://reference/{n}/save URL. That slot then reads as e.g. "Mix — 85 dB SPL" in Lyra's menu and HUD, and you can jump to it with Ctrl+F-key. Requires Lyra 1.4 or later.
