Audita

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Overview

Audita is a local macOS utility. It does not transmit, upload, or share any personal data or audio. All measurements and session history are stored only on your Mac. There are no analytics, no crash reporting, and no external network connections made by the app during normal use.

Microphone access

Audita requests Microphone access in System Settings. This permission is used solely to measure sound pressure levels from your chosen audio input device. Audio is processed entirely on-device in real time - raw audio samples are never recorded to disk, never transmitted, and are discarded after each measurement tick. Only derived numeric values (SPL in dBA/dBC, dose percentage) are retained.

Session data

Session history - including start/end times, dose percentages, average and peak SPL, and DAW names - is stored locally at:

~/Library/Containers/com.headroom.Audita/Data/Library/Application Support/Audita/sessions.json

This file never leaves your device. Sessions older than 90 days are pruned automatically on launch. The retention threshold (and a separate detailed-time-series retention) is user-configurable in Settings → General → Storage.

Session reports (PDF)

When you open a session report, Audita renders a PDF to a temporary location at ~/Library/Caches/Audita Reports/. The folder lives inside the standard macOS Caches directory; macOS manages cleanup on its own schedule. You can also empty it by hand at any time. The PDF is generated locally and only leaves your Mac if you actively share or save it through the macOS Share menu.

Preferences and settings

Your settings - selected input device, calibration offset, DAW list, dose preferences, retention thresholds, and notification settings - are stored locally on your Mac and are never transmitted.

Automation surfaces

Audita ships an audita:// URL scheme and a bundle of App Intents for Shortcuts, Siri, and Focus filters. These are local automation surfaces. URL responses use x-callback-url, which sends the response payload to a URL you supply. Audita does not contact any remote service through these surfaces; if your callback URL points to a third party, that's your choice and that party's policy then applies. The intents and URL scheme are read-and-toggle only: they cannot change calibration, license state, or A/C-weighting coefficients.

Lyra integration

When you click Send to Lyra… after a calibration, Audita opens a lyra:// URL on the same Mac to hand a slot number, a name (e.g. "K-14"), and a dB SPL value to Lyra. Nothing leaves your device. The handoff happens entirely between two local apps via macOS's URL handler.

Auto-update checks

Audita uses Sparkle for automatic updates. Sparkle periodically fetches the appcast feed at https://headroomstudio.dev/audita/appcast.xml to check for new versions. This request includes your macOS version and CPU architecture so Sparkle can determine compatibility, but no personal data is transmitted. You can disable automatic checks in the app's menu.

Purchases

Payments are processed by Lemon Squeezy, an external payment provider. When you purchase Audita, Lemon Squeezy's privacy policy applies to the transaction. Headroom does not receive or store your payment details. You can review Lemon Squeezy's privacy policy at lemonsqueezy.com/privacy.

Your data rights

If you're an EU or UK resident, you have rights under GDPR to access, rectify, port, or delete personal data we hold about you. In practice, Audita holds no personal data on its own: all session and SPL data lives locally on your Mac, and the only personal data linked to you anywhere is what Lemon Squeezy receives at checkout (email address, billing info). To exercise any of these rights, email hello@headroomstudio.dev and we'll route the request to Lemon Squeezy where applicable.

Uninstalling Audita

To remove Audita and its local data:

  1. Quit Audita from the menu bar.
  2. Drag Audita from /Applications to the Trash.
  3. To also remove session history and settings: delete ~/Library/Containers/com.headroom.Audita.

Your license key (if you've activated) survives this and reactivates a fresh install on the same Mac. To clear the license too, deactivate it first via Menu → Manage License → Deactivate before uninstalling.
