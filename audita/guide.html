§ 1

Quick start #

  1. Launch Audita. Grant microphone / audio input permission when prompted — it's how macOS lets Audita read your input level. No audio is ever recorded.
  2. Pick your measurement input in Settings (any CoreAudio device or channel).
  3. Watch the Pressure Bloom in your menu bar — live SPL plus today's dose tier at a glance.
  4. Run the target-level calibration to pin your monitor controller to a known dBA reference, then let Monitoring Discipline keep you honest.
§ 2

Microphone & input #

Audita reads from any CoreAudio input device or individual channel. For meaningful absolute SPL, use a calibrated measurement mic and load a mic-correction profile so the reading reflects the room rather than the capsule's response. Without calibration the figures are relative — still useful for tracking consistency and trends.

ⓘ

Audio is analysed in real time to derive a level number. It is never recorded, saved, or transmitted — nothing leaves your Mac.

§ 3

Reading the menu bar #

The Pressure Bloom shows two things at once: live SPL (the arc count) and today's accumulated dose tier (the colour). You can also show a live numeric SPL — dBA, dBC, or alongside the dose icon — for an at-a-glance reading without opening the popover.

TierMeaning
GreenSafe — comfortably within today's dose budget
YellowCaution — over half of the daily dose used
OrangeWarning — approaching the 100% daily dose
RedOver-exposed — past the NIOSH daily limit
§ 4

Target-level calibration #

The pink-noise wizard walks you through setting your monitor controller to a specific dBA target. Audita records the knob-position note, the target value, and the measured SPL — one calibration per output device. From then on, Monitoring Discipline knows where "on target" is, and you can hand the position to Lyra.

§ 5

Monitoring Discipline #

A live measure of how consistently you actually monitor at your calibrated reference. It shows as an on-target % chip in the menu bar with a green / yellow / orange ladder, plus a three-segment under / on / over bar. Opt-in monitoring alerts notify you when your level drifts (rolling 30-min Leq vs the session baseline) or sustains a deviation off target, with independent snooze per category.

§ 6

Hearing dose & NIOSH #

Audita accumulates daily exposure using the NIOSH REL: 85 dBA reference, 3 dB exchange rate, 80 dBA accumulation threshold. Peak monitoring follows EU Directive 2003/10/EC — C-weighted peaks at the 135 / 137 / 140 dBC action values, and A-weighted instantaneous peaks above the 115 dBA ceiling, counted per session and per day.

Conservative mode accumulates dose using max(dBA, dBC) — turn it on for bass-heavy work where low-frequency energy contributes more exposure than A-weighting credits.

§ 7

Leq & Ln statistics #

Rolling Leq over 1, 10, and 30 minutes plus L10 / L50 / L90 percentiles describe the character of a session at a finer grain than instantaneous level — how loud it's been on average, and how much it varies.

§ 8

Sessions, history & reports #

Audita auto-detects when you open a DAW (15 supported out of the box — Luna, Logic, Pro Tools, Ableton, REAPER, and more) and starts a session; you can also start and end manually. Each session records its dose contribution, average and peak SPL, and peak impulse counts.

The history window shows any date range with cumulative dose, daily average, EU weekly exposure level (L_EX,w), and peaks. Every session also generates a one-page PDF report — SPL chart with monitoring-discipline shading and a stats grid — from the history menu or the session-recap notification.

§ 9

Lyra pairing #

After a target-level calibration, click "Send to Lyra…" and choose a slot. Audita writes the calibrated dB SPL into one of Lyra's three reference slots via lyra://reference/{n}/save. The slot then reads as e.g. "Mix — 85 dB SPL" in Lyra's menu and HUD, recallable with Ctrl+F10/F11/F12. Requires Lyra 1.4 or later.

§ 10
